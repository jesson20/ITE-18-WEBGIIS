The Philippine Mangrove Forest

Top 5 Mangrove Provinces in the Philippines

Palawan

Palawan is known for its rich biodiversity, and its mangrove forests are no exception. With over 58,400 hectares of mangrove forests, Palawan has the highest mangrove cover in the Philippines. These forests are found along the province's coastline and provide a critical habitat for a variety of marine species, including fish, shrimp, and crabs. Mangrove forests also play an important role in protecting coastal communities from storm surges and erosion.

Palawan boasts a remarkable diversity of mangrove species, with over 31 species documented within its mangrove forests. These species include:

Rhizophora apiculata
Rhizophora mucronata
Ceriops tagal
Xylocarpus granatum
Bruguiera gymnorrhiza
Sonneratia alba
Rhizophora stylosa
Heritiera littoralis
Bruguiera cylindrica
Ceriops decandra

Misamis Occidental

Misamis Occidental, a province in the Philippines, is known for its rich natural resources, including extensive mangrove forests. With over 10,000 hectares of mangrove forests, Misamis Occidental plays a crucial role in maintaining coastal ecosystems and supporting a diverse range of marine life. These forests are found along the province's coastline and provide essential habitat for fish, shrimp, crabs, and other marine organisms, contributing significantly to the province's fisheries productivity. Additionally, these forests act as natural barriers against storm surges and erosion, protecting coastal communities from the damaging effects of typhoons and flooding.

Misamis Occidental boasts a remarkable diversity of mangrove species, with over 25 species documented within its mangrove forests. These species include:

Rhizophora apiculata
Rhizophora mucronata
Ceriops tagal
Xylocarpus granatum
Bruguiera gymnorrhiza
Sonneratia alba
Avicennia marina
Avicennia officinalis
Lumnitzera racemosa
Aegiceras corniculatum
