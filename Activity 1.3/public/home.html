The Philippine Mangrove Forest

A WEB-GIS Project of ITE-18 GROUP 2

Caraga State University - Main, Ampayon, Butuan City, Agusan del Norte, Philippines


Mangroves are unique coastal ecosystems characterized by a variety of salt-tolerant trees, shrubs, and other vegetation. They thrive in the intertidal zones along tropical and subtropical coastlines, where they are subjected to regular tidal inundation. These remarkable ecosystems provide numerous ecological, economic, and social benefits.

Mangroves have a long and storied history, deeply intertwined with human civilizations and the natural world. They are thought to have originated around 75 million years ago, with their ancestors evolving in the late Cretaceous period. Over time, mangroves have adapted to the harsh conditions of coastal environments, developing specialized root systems to anchor themselves in loose, oxygen-poor soils and filter salt from seawater.

In the Philippines, mangroves have played a significant role in the nation's cultural and ecological tapestry. These coastal forests have been utilized by local communities for centuries, providing resources such as timber, firewood, and fisheries support. However, the importance of preserving these critical ecosystems has gained recognition in recent years due to their role in coastal protection, carbon sequestration, and biodiversity conservation.

Now, let's delve into the top five provinces in the Philippines known for their extensive mangrove habitats.


Top 5 Mangrove Provinces in the Philippines

Palawan

Palawan is known for its rich biodiversity, and its mangrove forests are no exception. With over 58,400 hectares of mangrove forests, Palawan has the highest mangrove cover in the Philippines. These forests are found along the province's coastline and provide a critical habitat for a variety of marine species, including fish, shrimp, and crabs. Mangrove forests also play an important role in protecting coastal communities from storm surges and erosion.

Palawan boasts a remarkable diversity of mangrove species, with over 31 species documented within its mangrove forests. These species include:

Rhizophora apiculata
Rhizophora mucronata
Ceriops tagal
Xylocarpus granatum
Bruguiera gymnorrhiza
Sonneratia alba
Rhizophora stylosa
Heritiera littoralis
Bruguiera cylindrica
Ceriops decandra

Misamis Occidental

Misamis Occidental, a province in the Philippines, is known for its rich natural resources, including extensive mangrove forests. With over 10,000 hectares of mangrove forests, Misamis Occidental plays a crucial role in maintaining coastal ecosystems and supporting a diverse range of marine life. These forests are found along the province's coastline and provide essential habitat for fish, shrimp, crabs, and other marine organisms, contributing significantly to the province's fisheries productivity. Additionally, these forests act as natural barriers against storm surges and erosion, protecting coastal communities from the damaging effects of typhoons and flooding.
